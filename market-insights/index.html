How to Read This

Numbers without context are noise.

Every county snapshot below shows four metrics: how much inventory is on the market compared to a year ago, how much buyer demand exists, how many homes actually closed, and how long the average home sits before going under contract. These four numbers, read together, tell you whether your county is a seller's market, a buyer's market, or somewhere in between.

If you want to know what these numbers mean for your specific home, that's the conversation worth having on a call.

What This Means for Sellers

Inventory is up but demand is flat, which means more sellers competing for the same pool of buyers. Closed sales are down 7%, and homes are taking nearly a week longer to go under contract than they were a year ago. For a seller in LA County, this is the moment where pricing strategy matters most. List too high and you'll join the homes sitting past 100 days. Price right and you'll still find the buyer faster than your competition.

What This Means for Sellers

Orange County remains the tightest market of the four, with a 70-day expected market time well below LA, Riverside, and San Bernardino. Inventory is climbing slightly and closed sales are softening, but demand is holding steady. For sellers, this is still a healthy market, but the days of homes flying off the shelf in two weeks are behind us. Strategic pricing and strong presentation matter more now than they did a year ago.

What This Means for Sellers

Riverside County is the only one of the four where inventory is shrinking. Demand is also down slightly, which keeps the market roughly balanced. Expected market time is essentially unchanged from last year. For sellers, this is a stable, predictable market. The opportunity here is positioning your home as the cleaner option in a smaller pool of inventory. Less competition means less reason to compete on price if your home is well-prepared.

What This Means for Sellers

San Bernardino County is the most stable of the four, with inventory and market time both essentially unchanged from a year ago. Demand and closed sales are down slightly. For sellers, the playbook here is patience and pricing discipline. With a 105-day expected market time, the homes that sell quickly are the ones that come to market priced correctly on day one and presented in the top 25% of their price band.
